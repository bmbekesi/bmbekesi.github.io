Balázs Márk Békési

Currently I am a PhD student at the Leibniz University Hannover under the guidance of Lynn Heller and Roger Bielawski. Beforehand, I studied mathematics and some theoretical physics at the LUH.

Research profile

I am broadly interested in several aspects of geometry and mathematical physics. Some of my current projects are:

  • Non-existence theorems for harmonic maps.
  • Understanding the structure of the moduli space of the extended Bogomolny equation.
  • Universal Higgs bundles and properties of canonical sections.
  • Deformations of the character variety of the once punctured torus.
